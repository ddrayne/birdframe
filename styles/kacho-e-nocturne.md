# kacho-e-nocturne

## Collection
Print Traditions

## Description
An intimate bird-and-flower nocturne: a branch, a voice, Edinburgh weather, and all the eloquence of what is left unprinted.

## Lineage
Japanese kachō-e bird-and-flower woodblock prints of the Edo and Meiji periods

## Medium
colour woodblock with bokashi and mica on washi

## Palette
Prussian blue, sumi, moon silver, muted russet, paper white

## Affinities
night-active, dusk-heavy, sparse, dominant-species, rain, snow, fog

## Source
https://www.adachi-hanga.com/ukiyo-e-en/category/6

## Prompt
A refined Japanese kachō-e bird-and-flower woodblock nocturne depicting {scene}. One or a few accurately observed birds, an eloquent branch or stem, Edinburgh rain or moon atmosphere, carved contour, restrained colour blocks, subtle bokashi where historically appropriate, mica-like night shimmer and powerful unprinted space. Portrait ōban feeling.

## Avoid
anime, photographic feather texture, generic Mount Fuji, crowded composition, modern text, signatures, watermarks
