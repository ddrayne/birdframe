# bewick-wood-engraving

## Collection
British Naturalists

## Description
A tiny world cut in black and white, where feather, bark, weather, and human-scale landscape emerge from exquisite marks.

## Lineage
Thomas Bewick and the British wood-engraved natural histories of the late eighteenth century

## Medium
end-grain wood engraving printed in black on aged wove paper

## Palette
lamp black, warm ivory, paper foxing

## Affinities
sparse, dominant-species, winter, wind, night-active, first-arrival

## Source
https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/353598

## Prompt
A late eighteenth-century British natural-history wood engraving depicting {scene}. Extremely fine white-line cutting in dense black ink, tactile cross-hatching, minute accurate feather marks, a small narrative Edinburgh landscape vignette beneath or around the birds, and broad untouched paper. Portrait orientation, materially convincing as an end-grain block impression.

## Avoid
colour except paper tone, smooth vector lines, photographic shading, modern typography, decorative border, watermarks
