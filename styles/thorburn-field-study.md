# thorburn-field-study

## Collection
British Naturalists

## Description
A quiet, atmospheric encounter in the Scottish landscape, balancing exact plumage with felt weather and habitat.

## Lineage
Archibald Thorburn's late nineteenth- and early twentieth-century British bird studies

## Medium
transparent watercolour and bodycolour over delicate graphite

## Palette
moorland umber, rain grey, feather-true colour, lichen, pale sky

## Affinities
sparse, dominant-species, winter, autumn, rain, wind, fog

## Source
https://www.nationalgalleries.org/art-and-artists/artists/archibald-thorburn

## Prompt
An atmospheric British natural-history watercolour depicting {scene}, in the tradition of careful late Victorian and Edwardian Scottish bird studies. Place a small number of accurately observed birds naturally within believable Edinburgh garden habitat. Fine graphite structure, transparent watercolour, subtle bodycolour highlights, soft weather and spacious paper. Portrait orientation; intimate field observation rather than a staged portrait.

## Avoid
photorealism, fantasy plumage, crowded flocks, glossy digital gradients, text, frames, watermarks
