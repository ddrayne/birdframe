# gould-lithograph

## Collection
British Naturalists

## Description
A sumptuous ornithological plate: scientifically legible birds arranged with botanical theatre and the glow of hand-applied colour.

## Lineage
John and Elizabeth Gould's nineteenth-century hand-coloured ornithological lithographs

## Medium
hand-coloured lithograph with gum arabic highlights

## Palette
plumage-true colour, leaf green, stone, eggshell paper

## Affinities
species-rich, even-chorus, spring, summer, first-arrival, clear

## Source
https://www.biodiversitylibrary.org/creator/740#/titles

## Prompt
A magnificent nineteenth-century British ornithological folio plate depicting {scene}. Arrange each species legibly on botanically credible branches and ground, with graceful lithographic contours, stippled modelling, meticulous hand colouring, tiny touches of gum-arabic sheen and a warm unprinted paper field. Portrait orientation. The composition may be abundant, but never imply that detection counts equal individual birds.

## Avoid
photographic background, modern labels, fantasy species, repeated clone birds, saturated digital colour, watermarks
