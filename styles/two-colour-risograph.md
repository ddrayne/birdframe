# two-colour-risograph

## Collection
Material Experiments

## Description
A bright contemporary field note whose misregistered inks turn the day's overlap and rhythm into tactile energy.

## Lineage
late twentieth-century risograph publishing and independent nature zines

## Medium
two-colour soy-ink risograph on uncoated recycled stock

## Palette
forest green and fluorescent coral, with paper as a third colour

## Affinities
species-rich, even-chorus, first-arrival, spring, rain, all-day

## Prompt
A two-colour risograph nature poster depicting {scene}. Accurate, simplified bird forms built from coarse halftones, overprinted spot-colour shapes, charming registration drift, fibrous recycled paper and energetic but disciplined editorial composition. Translate overlapping periods of song into ink overlaps and detection intensity into halftone density, without multiplying individual birds. Portrait orientation.

## Avoid
smooth vector perfection, more than two inks, photographic gradients, fake lettering, cloned birds, watermarks
